Context

The Opportunity

Travel insurance is a natural complement to flight bookings — yet at MMT GCC/Global, insurance products were completely decoupled from the booking flow. Users had to actively seek them out, resulting in near-zero organic attach rates.

Simultaneously, there was a broader business mandate to grow ancillary revenue. Embedded finance — surfacing financial products at the moment of highest intent — was the clearest path.

Opportunity Statement Embed Trip Delay & Baggage Insurance natively into the booking flow to capture purchase intent at its peak — right before payment confirmation.

Problem Definition

Why Wasn't This Done Before?

🔗

Decoupled UX

Insurance was a separate product page — no handoff from the booking funnel to insurance discovery.

📋

No Business Case

Revenue potential hadn't been formally modelled. No one had quantified what attach rate improvement was worth.

⚙️

Technical Debt

Booking flow had limited slots for new UI elements — required careful placement to avoid conversion regression.

🤝

Partner Alignment

Insurance provider integration required API alignment, claims workflow definition, and legal sign-off.

03 / 10
Strategy

Two-Track Approach

01

Embedded Insurance UX

Design and ship Trip Delay & Baggage Insurance cards natively within the booking confirmation step. Placement optimised for visibility without disrupting core booking conversion. A/B tested opt-in vs opt-out framing.

02

SQL-Driven Recommendation Engine

Built a recommendation engine using booking and post-sales behavioural signals — route risk (delay-prone airports), traveller profile (frequent flyer vs first-timer), and historical claims data — to surface the right insurance product to the right user at the right moment.

04 / 10
Execution

Building the Business Case First

Business Case Modelling

  • Modelled attach rate scenarios: 5%, 10%, 15% of booking volume
  • Calculated revenue per attached policy vs CAC
  • Mapped to annual GCC/Global booking volume
  • Presented to leadership with break-even and upside scenarios

Recommendation Engine

  • Defined feature set: route, airport delay history, traveller tier
  • Wrote SQL queries to extract and validate training signals
  • Worked with DS to build scoring model
  • Integrated scores into booking flow API response

UX Integration

  • Mapped 3 candidate placement slots in booking flow
  • A/B tested pre-payment vs post-selection placement
  • Tested opt-in checkbox vs pre-selected toggle
  • Monitored core booking conversion as guardrail metric
Key Risk Managed Core booking conversion could not regress. This was the non-negotiable guardrail metric throughout all testing phases.

Learnings

Key Takeaways

L1

Business Case Unlocks Resources

The feature sat in the backlog until I modelled the revenue potential. A clear ₹ number attached to the initiative moved it to the top of the priority stack. Always quantify before pitching.

L2

Guardrail Metrics Are Non-Negotiable

Setting core booking conversion as a hard guardrail — not just a soft KPI — created alignment across engineering, design, and leadership on what "success" meant.

L3

Timing > Placement

The pre-payment placement outperformed post-selection by 2x in A/B testing. Intent is highest right before the user commits money — that's the moment to surface the add-on.

07 / 10
Technical Depth

The Recommendation Engine

Signal Sources

  • Route signals: Historical delay rates by airport pair
  • Traveller signals: Booking frequency, tier, past claims
  • Session signals: Trip duration, # of passengers, fare class
  • Post-sales signals: Prior baggage issues, cancellation history

Output

A relevance score (0–1) per user-session-product combination, surfaced via booking flow API. Higher scores triggered proactive insurance card placement; lower scores showed a softer CTA.

My Role in the Engine I defined the feature set and wrote the initial SQL queries to validate signal quality. The model architecture was built by DS — my contribution was making sure the right inputs went in and the right product decision came out.
Data Stack SQL (Redshift) · Internal booking events · Post-sales claims database · Mixpanel for conversion tracking
08 / 10
Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

📊

Test More Variants

We tested 2 placement variants. With more runway I'd test 4–5 — including post-booking email nudge and in-app notification 24hrs before departure.

🌍

Localise for GCC

GCC travellers have different insurance needs (visa requirements, medical coverage). A region-specific product variant could unlock a materially higher attach rate.

🔄

Post-Purchase Retargeting

The attach rate window doesn't close at booking. A lifecycle journey (WhatsApp nudge T-48hrs) targeting unattached bookers would compound the revenue impact.
