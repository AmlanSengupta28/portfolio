Problem Statement

The Brief

The Delhi Metro serves 2.5M+ daily passengers across 390+ km — yet its digital touchpoints are fragmented, impersonal, and disconnected from the expectations of Gen Z commuters (18–25).

Gen Z prioritises affordability, real-time information, social connectivity, and eco-conscious choices. The current DMRC app addresses none of these in an integrated way.

Problem Statement Enhance the Delhi Metro experience for Gen Z commuters by addressing gaps in information access, personalisation, and engagement through a mobile app.

User Research & Personas

Primary Research

  • Surveyed 37 individuals aged 18–23 across demographics
  • Conducted 2 in-depth interviews
  • Pain point word cloud analysis
  • Rated: Value for money, Safety, Wait time, Punctuality

Gen Z Behavioural Insights

  • 80% prioritise affordability in public transport
  • 60% prioritise convenience
  • 70% prefer eco-friendly options

Monetization

Revenue Model

Free Tier

  • Real-time metro updates
  • Social trip planning with friends
  • Basic CO₂ emission tracking
  • Nearby attractions & services

Premium Tier — ₹99/mo · ₹999/yr

  • Ad-free experience

  • Priority community features

Additional Revenue Streams

  • Geo-targeted advertising from nearby businesses
  • Commission on parcel pick/drop services

Go-To-Market

Launch Strategy

Target Audience

Partnerships

07 / 10
Metrics

Success Framework

NSM

North Star: Weekly Active Users (WAU)

High WAU = users are integrating the app into their daily commute. It captures both acquisition success and retention quality. Target: 100K WAU within 6 months of launch.

APP

Application Metrics

Downloads and acquisition-to-active user ratio (quality of top-of-funnel). User engagement rate (% of downloads actively using core features weekly). Session duration vs metro ride duration.

NEG

Negative / Guardrail Metrics

Churn rate (weekly uninstallations) and user complaints per week. If churn exceeds 5%/week or complaints spike — stop new feature releases and fix core issues first.

08 / 10
Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

🔐

Safety-First Design

Vandana's safety concern was the #1 pain point but we didn't build a dedicated safety feature. A live-location sharing with trusted contacts feature would directly address this and drive female user retention.

🎮

Gamify the Commute

The Community Hub was generic. I'd design a commute-specific gamification layer — daily trivia, metro riddle of the day, leaderboards — to turn a boring 36 minutes into a ritual.

💳

Freemium Threshold

₹99/mo might be too high a jump from free. I'd test a ₹29/mo micro-premium tier first — lower barrier, validates willingness to pay before investing in the full premium buildout.
