Problem

The Fuel Station Crisis

India's 83,000+ fuel stations face an existential threat. As EV adoption accelerates, petrol and diesel volumes are projected to decline sharply over the next decade — but no structured transition framework exists for station operators.

The challenge: how do you enable fuel stations to transition to EV charging without requiring massive upfront capital, while creating a sustainable recurring revenue model?

Challenge Brief Design a product that enables fuel stations to transition to EV mobility infrastructure while maintaining capital viability.

Insight

Why Fuel Stations Are the Right Infrastructure

📍

Location Advantage

Fuel stations already sit at high-traffic road nodes — exactly where EV charging demand will be highest.

⚡

Power Infrastructure

Existing high-capacity electrical connections at fuel stations reduce the cost of EV charger installation significantly.

🏪

Dwell Time = Revenue

EV charging takes 20–45 mins vs 5 mins for fuel — creating new in-station retail and services revenue opportunity.

👷

Existing Workforce

Station operators and attendants can be retrained to manage charging operations — no net new workforce needed.

Product Design

The Platform Model

01

Subscription-Based Access

EV drivers subscribe to a monthly/annual plan — similar to a fuel loyalty card. Subscription provides priority access, reserved charging slots, and discounted per-kWh rates at any partner station. This creates predictable recurring revenue for station operators.

02

Dynamic Pricing Engine

Non-subscribed drivers pay dynamic rates based on time of day, station utilisation, and grid electricity cost. Peak hours (7–10am, 5–8pm) priced at premium. Off-peak priced at cost + margin. Maximises revenue per charger per day.

03

Station Operator Dashboard

Station owners see real-time utilisation, revenue per charger, subscriber base, and maintenance alerts. Platform takes a commission; operators retain the majority of revenue. Capital for charger installation provided via lease model — zero upfront for operators.

Unit Economics

Financial Model

Revenue Per Asset (Annual)

  • Subscription revenue: 50 subscribers/station × ₹999/mo × 12 = ₹5.99L
  • Dynamic pricing (pay-per-use): 20 sessions/day × ₹150 avg × 365 = ₹10.95L
  • In-station retail uplift: ₹1L est. (conservative)
  • Platform commission (20%): ₹3.19L retained
Net Revenue to Station Operator ~₹10L per asset per year after platform commission

Cost Structure Per Asset

  • Charger + installation: ₹6L (leased, ₹1.2L/yr over 5 yrs)
  • Grid upgrade: ₹2L one-time (amortised)
  • Maintenance: ₹0.8L/yr
  • Platform fee: 20% of revenue
Breakeven ~1.21 years per asset — validated at competition

Go-To-Market

Launch Strategy

Phase 1: Pilot (0–6 months)

  • Partner with 10 fuel stations in 2 metro cities (Delhi, Bengaluru)
  • Target highway corridors with high EV traffic
  • Offer operators zero-cost installation in exchange for 2-year exclusivity
  • Build subscriber base through early-adopter pricing

Phase 2: Scale (6–18 months)

  • Expand to 100 stations across top-10 EV cities
  • Integrate with OEM apps (Tata Nexon, Ola Electric) for discovery
  • Launch corporate fleet subscription tier
Key Partnership IOCL / HPCL as anchor partners — they operate 50K+ stations and have a mandate to transition. A white-label deal gives instant distribution at scale.
Regulatory Tailwind FAME III subsidies and BEE mandates for public charging infrastructure create a favourable policy environment for the first 24 months.

Risks

Risk & Mitigation

R1

Slow EV Adoption = Low Utilisation

Mitigation: Model assumes 20% EV market share by 2027 (conservative). Even at 10%, the subscription revenue base covers fixed costs. Dynamic pricing floor ensures margin protection at low utilisation.

R2

Operator Reluctance to Transition

Mitigation: Zero upfront capital via lease model removes the biggest barrier. Revenue sharing starts from day 1. Pilot data from Phase 1 stations creates social proof for wider rollout.

R3

Competition from Pure-Play Networks

Mitigation: Pure-play networks (Tata Power EV, Ather Grid) are building greenfield — our fuel station assets have location and infrastructure advantages that are 5–7 years ahead in amortisation.

Reflection

What I'd Sharpen

📱

App-First Experience

The product needs a strong consumer app — slot booking, charge tracking, payment. I'd invest more in the consumer UX design in V2 of this proposal.

🔋

Battery Swapping Option

For two-wheelers (Ola, Ather) a battery swap model would complement charging — higher throughput, shorter dwell time. Worth modelling as a parallel revenue line.

📐

More Granular Unit Economics

City-level economics differ significantly. Bengaluru vs Tier-2 city utilisation rates would change the breakeven calculation — a sensitivity analysis by city tier would strengthen the model.
