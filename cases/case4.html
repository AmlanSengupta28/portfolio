The Simulation Brief

As part of the IIM Lucknow MBA curriculum, this project simulated a full product management engagement for Lenskart — India's largest omnichannel eyewear brand with 2,000+ stores and a leading D2C app.

The objective: act as a PM team, take a product area from discovery to sprint delivery — defining epics, writing user stories, prioritising a backlog, and executing structured sprint releases with measurable velocity.

Scope Focus area: improving Lenskart's online try-on and post-purchase experience — the highest friction zones in the customer journey.

Discovery

Understanding Lenskart's Product Gaps

🥽

Virtual Try-On Friction

The AR try-on feature had poor frame alignment accuracy and no prescription overlay — users couldn't make confident purchase decisions.

📦

Post-Purchase Drop-off

Order tracking was fragmented across app, email, and SMS — users contacted support excessively for delivery status.

🔁

Reorder Experience

Prescription reorders required re-entering full details each time — no saved prescription or frame history.

⭐

Review & Social Proof

Review system lacked photo uploads and prescription-specific ratings — limiting social proof for high-consideration purchases.

Epics & Backlog

5 Epics → 18 User Stories

E1

Enhanced Virtual Try-On

4 user stories — improved AR frame alignment, prescription power overlay, multi-frame comparison, and social sharing. Priority: Must-have. RICE score: 92.

E2

Unified Order Tracking

3 user stories — real-time delivery tracking in-app, proactive delay notifications, and one-tap support escalation. Priority: Must-have. RICE score: 88.

E3

Prescription Vault

4 user stories — saved prescription profiles, auto-fill on reorder, prescription expiry reminders, and optometrist integration. Priority: Should-have. RICE score: 74.

E4 / E5

Social Reviews + Loyalty

7 user stories across photo reviews, prescription-tagged ratings, and a loyalty points programme. Priority: Could-have. RICE scores: 65–70.

Framework

KPI Architecture

Activation Metrics

  • % of new users completing try-on in first session
  • % of users saving a prescription in first 7 days
  • Time-to-first-order from app install

Retention Metrics

  • 30-day reorder rate (prescription users vs non)
  • In-app order tracking engagement rate
  • Support contact rate per 100 orders (guardrail)

Revenue Metrics

  • Average order value: try-on users vs non-try-on
  • Prescription vault: reorder revenue per user
  • Loyalty programme: points redemption rate
North Star Weekly Prescription Reorder Rate — the best leading indicator of long-term LTV in an eyewear D2C business.

Learnings

What This Taught Me

L1

Epics Are Strategy, Stories Are Tactics

The best user stories I wrote were the ones where the epic gave a clear strategic intent ("reduce reorder friction") and the story gave a specific, testable outcome ("user can reorder in under 30 seconds from order history"). Blurring these levels creates undeliverable stories.

L2

RICE Without Validation Is Just Opinion

The RICE scores I assigned felt objective but were based on assumptions about reach and impact. In a real engagement, every score above 80 would need a data source or user research backing — otherwise it's just PM intuition dressed up as a framework.

L3

Estimation Accuracy Comes from Definition Quality

Our 96% accuracy wasn't luck — it came from writing precise acceptance criteria for each story before estimation. Vague stories get wildly varied estimates. Precise stories converge quickly.

08 / 10
Reflection

What I'd Do Differently in a Real Engagement

👥

User Research First

I identified pain points from secondary research. In a real PM role I'd run 10–15 user interviews before writing a single story — the epics might have looked completely different.

🔬

Validate RICE Scores

Each RICE reach estimate should be tied to actual analytics data — DAU/MAU, funnel drop-off rates, support ticket volume. Gut-feel scores are a starting point, not a final prioritisation.

📏

Define DoD More Rigorously

Our Definition of Done was loose. In a real sprint, I'd add explicit QA checkpoints, analytics instrumentation requirements, and stakeholder sign-off as DoD criteria for every story.
