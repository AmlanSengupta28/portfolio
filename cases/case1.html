Context

The Problem Space

MakeMyTrip GCC/Global was receiving high volumes of flight traffic through aggregator platforms (Kayak, Google Flights etc). While this drove top-of-funnel volume, the conversion to direct bookings was stuck at 10% — well below industry benchmarks.

This meant we were effectively paying acquisition costs to aggregators for users who then booked directly — a costly and inefficient loop.

Core Question How do we shift user intent from aggregator browsing to direct booking without sacrificing volume?

Diagnosis

Root Cause Analysis

🔍

No Intent Filtering

All aggregator traffic landed equally — no distinction between high and low booking intent users.

📊

Funnel Leakage

Round Trip listings had a stepped drop-off — users weren't seeing the right fare options at the right moment.

💸

Cost Inefficiency

CAC was inflated by low-intent hits that consumed infrastructure resources without converting.

🎯

Fare Visibility

Premium and business fare families had low interstitial visibility — users defaulted to economy without considering upgrades.

03 / 10
Strategy

Three-Track Approach

01

Traffic Gating Layer

Architected a gating mechanism to identify and filter ~10M daily low-intent aggregator hits using behavioural signals — time-on-page, session depth, prior booking history — while preserving high-intent traffic and booking conversion rates.

02

DS-Driven Predictive Clustering

Worked with Data Science to build a predictive clustering model for Round Trip flight listings. The model identified user intent clusters and served personalised fare sequences — driving step-wise conversion improvements at each funnel stage.

03

Interstitial Redesign

Redesigned fare-family interstitials to increase premium and business fare visibility. A/B tested placement, copy, and visual hierarchy — resulting in higher fare-family share without degrading overall conversion.

04 / 10
Execution

How It Was Built

Traffic Gating

  • Defined intent signals with Data Science — session depth, scroll patterns, prior booking signals
  • Built gating rules in collaboration with engineering
  • Monitored booking conversion vs hit reduction ratio weekly

Predictive Clustering

  • Defined feature set for clustering model with DS team
  • Ran controlled A/B tests on Round Trip listings
  • Iterated on cluster definitions based on conversion signals

Interstitial Redesign

  • Mapped full fare-selection UX flow with design team
  • Identified low-visibility zones for premium fare cards
  • A/B tested 3 variants — copy, placement, visual treatment
  • Shipped winning variant to 100% of GCC traffic
Tools Used SQL · Mixpanel · Figma · Internal A/B testing framework · Confluence PRDs

Learnings

What I Learned

L1

Intent ≠ Volume

High traffic numbers can mask poor intent quality. Filtering for intent — even at the cost of some volume — delivers better unit economics and cleaner conversion funnels.

L2

DS Models Need PM Guardrails

Predictive models are only as good as the metrics they optimise for. Defining the right north star (step-wise conversion vs overall booking rate) was the most important decision in the DS collaboration.

L3

UX Changes Have Monetization Upside

The interstitial redesign was framed as a "UX improvement" internally but had a direct monetization impact. Framing product work in revenue terms gets faster alignment from stakeholders.

08 / 10
Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

⚡

Move Faster on Gating

I was conservative about how aggressively to gate low-intent traffic. In hindsight, the data supported a more aggressive threshold from week 3 — I would act on signals faster.

📐

Define Success Earlier

The A/B test success metrics for the interstitial were defined mid-experiment. Next time I'd pre-register the primary metric and minimum detectable effect before launch.

🤝

Involve Finance Earlier

The ₹1.3 Cr savings figure was calculated post-launch. Involving finance in the business case earlier would have accelerated stakeholder buy-in and resource allocation.
